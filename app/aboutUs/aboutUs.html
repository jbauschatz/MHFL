Welcome to the MHFL!

The Midwest Historical Fencing League offers organized HEMA competition throughout the Midwest region. Our tournaments are officially adjudicated according to a variety of rule sets reflecting the diversity of HEMA tradition. Partnered with our proud sponsors, we offer high quality prizes for the top competitors.

What is HEMA?

HEMA is the acronym for Historic European Martial Arts, which encompasses the study and practice of martial arts developed within the European continent prior to the modern age. Our goal is simple: To be deadly efficient while using swords, pole-arms and bare hands. We take our lessons from manuscripts written by Masters dating back to 1300CE. HEMA learns from the Master's life experiences since living and dying by the sword and other Arts of War was their reality. The international HEMA community transcribes, interprets and then physically applies those written works. We drill, train and compete with other HEMA practitioners to recreate the true-to-form cold efficiency of these disciplines. HEMA is a growing movement with many facets of study available including armored fighting, two-handed or single-handed swords, pole-arms and wrestling. Though we ultimately use blunted steel weapons for realistic training, HEMA focuses on the safe and controlled application of proper technique. Historic European Martial Arts were a deadly practice, but we prefer not to injure our training partners and ensure that all participants use appropriate safety equipment for the activity.
